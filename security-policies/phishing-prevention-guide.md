## 1. What Is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers impersonate trusted organizations to trick employees into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords or financial data.

---

## 2. Common Signs of Phishing Emails

### **2.1 Suspicious Sender**
- Email address doesn’t match the organization  
- Misspelled domain names  

### **2.2 Urgent or Threatening Language**
- “Your account will be closed”  
- “Immediate action required”  

### **2.3 Unexpected Attachments**
- ZIP files  
- PDFs claiming to be invoices or reports  

### **2.4 Fake Links**
- Hovering reveals a different URL  
- Links to login pages that look “almost” real  

### **2.5 Poor Grammar or Formatting**
- Unusual tone  
- Spelling mistakes  

---

## 3. How to Verify an Email

- Hover over links before clicking  
- Check the sender’s domain  
- Look for inconsistencies in tone or formatting  
- Contact the sender through official channels  
- Never trust emails asking for passwords  

---

## 4. What To Do If You Suspect Phishing

1. **Do NOT click any links**  
2. **Do NOT download attachments**  
3. **Report the email immediately**  
4. **Delete the message**  
5. If you clicked the link, contact IT immediately  

---

## 5. Examples of Phishing Scenarios

### **Scenario 1: Fake IT Support Email**
- Claims your password is expiring  
- Includes a link to a fake login page  

### **Scenario 2: Fake Invoice**
- Appears to come from a vendor  
- Contains a malicious attachment  

### **Scenario 3: CEO Fraud**
- Attacker impersonates a senior executive  
- Requests urgent financial transfers  

---

## 6. Prevention Tips

- Use MFA  
- Verify before responding  
- Keep software updated  
- Avoid using personal email for work  
- Stay alert during holidays (phishing spikes)  

---

## 7. Conclusion

Phishing remains one of the most common cyber threats. Awareness and quick reporting are the strongest defenses. When in doubt, report it.
